# i-frame vs s-frame in Behavioral Public Policy

## Cite_Key
chater2022iframe

## Problem
Behavioral science has over-focused on individual-level interventions (i-frame), neglecting systemic solutions (s-frame), leading to modest policy results.

## Method
Conceptual analysis contrasting individual-level (i-frame) and systemic (s-frame) approaches to behavioral public policy, with retrospective critique of the nudge paradigm.

## Data
Review of behavioral intervention results, policy case studies, and meta-analyses of nudge effectiveness.

## Metrics
Effect sizes of individual-level behavioral interventions; qualitative comparison of i-frame vs s-frame policy outcomes.

## Findings
Individual-level behavioral interventions have yielded disappointingly modest results. The i-frame deflects attention from systemic solutions and may provide political cover for inaction on structural reform. Authors argue for rebalancing toward s-frame approaches.

## Limitations
Primarily theoretical argument; does not provide empirical head-to-head comparison of i-frame vs s-frame interventions; may understate contexts where individual-level interventions are appropriate.

## Citation
Chater & Loewenstein (2022). The i-frame and the s-frame. Behav Brain Sci. doi:10.1017/s0140525x22002023
