# Caregiver Burden and Mental Health During COVID-19

## Cite_Key
russell2020initial

## Problem
COVID-19 pandemic imposed sudden caregiving challenges; understanding initial impacts on caregiver burden, mental health, and parent-child relationships.

## Method
Survey-based study of caregivers during early COVID-19 pandemic assessing burden, mental health outcomes, and relationship quality.

## Data
Caregiver self-report data collected during early pandemic period (abstract details unavailable).

## Metrics
Caregiver burden scales, mental health indicators, parent-child relationship quality measures.

## Findings
Documents initial caregiving challenges posed by COVID-19, including elevated caregiver burden and mental health strain, with implications for parent-child relationship dynamics.

## Limitations
Abstract unavailable for detailed methodology; cross-sectional design limits causal inference; early pandemic snapshot may not reflect longer-term adaptation.

## Citation
Russell et al. (2020). Initial Challenges of Caregiving During COVID-19. Child Psychiatry Hum Dev. doi:10.1007/s10578-020-01037-x
