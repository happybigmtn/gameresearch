# Data Feminism: Intersectional Ethics for Data Science

## Cite_Key
dignazio2020data

## Problem
Data science narratives are overwhelmingly white, male, and techno-heroic; the field lacks frameworks that address power, justice, and intersectional equity.

## Method
Theoretical framework grounded in intersectional feminist thought, illustrated with case studies of data feminism in action.

## Data
Case studies spanning data-driven injustice exposure, health outcomes, surveillance, discrimination, and civic participation.

## Metrics
Qualitative; no quantitative metrics. Evaluates data practices against equity and justice principles.

## Findings
Proposes a feminist framework for data science ethics that foregrounds questions of power: who collects data, for whom, and with whose interests. Demonstrates how data can both expose injustice and perpetuate harm.

## Limitations
Primarily theoretical/qualitative; operationalizing feminist data principles in technical systems remains an open challenge; Western-centric framing.

## Citation
D'Ignazio & Klein (2020). Data Feminism. MIT Press. doi:10.7551/mitpress/11805.001.0001
