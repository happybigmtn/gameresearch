# Trustworthy AI: From Principles to Regulation

## Cite_Key
diazrodriguez2023connecting

## Problem
Trustworthy AI requires meeting technical, ethical, and legal requirements throughout the system lifecycle, but existing frameworks lack holistic integration.

## Method
Conceptual framework connecting four axes: global ethical principles, philosophical AI ethics, risk-based regulation, and seven technical requirements (human agency, transparency, fairness, etc.).

## Data
Analysis of AI ethics guidelines, regulatory frameworks (EU AI Act context), and published trustworthy AI research.

## Metrics
Qualitative assessment across seven requirements: human agency/oversight, technical robustness, privacy, transparency, fairness, societal wellbeing, accountability.

## Findings
Proposes a holistic vision for trustworthy AI that goes beyond technical requirements to encompass all processes and actors in the AI lifecycle. Maps connections between principles, ethics, requirements, and regulation as four essential axes.

## Limitations
Primarily conceptual; operationalization of holistic trustworthiness remains challenging; regulatory landscape continues evolving post-publication.

## Citation
Díaz-Rodríguez et al. (2023). Connecting the dots in trustworthy AI. Inf Fusion. doi:10.1016/j.inffus.2023.101896
