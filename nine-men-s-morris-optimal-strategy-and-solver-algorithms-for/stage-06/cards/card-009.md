# Approved Oligonucleotide Therapeutics: Chemistry, Structure, Function

## Cite_Key
egli2023chemistry

## Problem
Eighteen oligonucleotide therapeutics approved over 25 years span diverse mechanisms; a unified review of their chemistry and clinical applications is needed.

## Method
Comprehensive review of approved nucleic acid therapeutics covering chemical modifications, mechanisms of action (ASOs, SSOs, RNAi, aptamers), and clinical applications.

## Data
All 18 approved oligonucleotide drugs as of publication, including their chemical structures, modification patterns, and clinical indications.

## Metrics
Qualitative comparison of modification chemistries (2'-fluoro, 2'-O-methoxyethyl, phosphorothioate, etc.) and their pharmacological properties.

## Findings
Only a handful of first- and second-generation chemical modifications underpin all approved oligonucleotide drugs. Targets include familial hypercholesterolemia, SMA, DMD, hereditary ATTR amyloidosis, porphyria, and hyperoxaluria. Chemical modification was central to making viable drugs from oligonucleotides.

## Limitations
Focuses on approved drugs only; emerging modification chemistries in clinical trials not fully covered; limited discussion of delivery challenges and tissue targeting.

## Citation
Egli & Manoharan (2023). Chemistry, structure and function of approved oligonucleotide therapeutics. Nucleic Acids Res. doi:10.1093/nar/gkad067
