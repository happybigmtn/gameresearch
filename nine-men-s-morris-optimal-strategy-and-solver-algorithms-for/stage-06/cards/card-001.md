# US Cancer Incidence and Mortality Trends Through 2021

## Cite_Key
siegel2021cancer

## Problem
Need for annual updated estimates of new cancer cases and deaths in the United States to guide public health priorities.

## Method
Compilation of incidence data (through 2017) from SEER, NPCR, and NAACCR; mortality data (through 2018) from NCHS; statistical projection for 2021.

## Data
National cancer registry incidence data (through 2017); NCHS mortality data (through 2018); US population-based cancer occurrence records.

## Metrics
Projected new cases, projected deaths, percent decline in cancer death rate from 1991 peak.

## Findings
Projected 1,898,160 new cancer cases and 608,570 deaths in the US in 2021. Cancer death rate fell continuously from 1991 peak through 2018, totaling a 31% decline, driven by reduced smoking and improved early detection and treatment.

## Limitations
Projections rely on historical trends and may not capture recent disruptions (e.g., COVID-19 screening delays). Incidence data lags by ~4 years; mortality by ~3 years.

## Citation
Siegel et al. (2021). Cancer Statistics, 2021. CA: A Cancer Journal for Clinicians. doi:10.3322/caac.21654
