# Sex and Gender Bias in Biomedical AI

## Cite_Key
cirillo2020gender

## Problem
Most biomedical AI technologies do not account for sex and gender bias, leading to algorithms that ignore inter-individual differences critical for precision medicine.

## Method
Review and analysis of how sex and gender dimensions are (not) incorporated into biomedical AI algorithm design, training data, and validation.

## Data
Published biomedical AI studies, clinical datasets, and algorithmic fairness literature with focus on sex/gender representation.

## Metrics
Qualitative assessment of bias detection mechanisms, sex/gender stratification in training data, and algorithmic performance disparities.

## Findings
Majority of biomedical AI algorithms ignore sex and gender dimensions, contributing to biased health outcomes. Failure to account for these differences undermines precision medicine goals. Calls for systematic integration of sex/gender variables in AI development pipelines.

## Limitations
Primarily review-based without novel empirical benchmarks; sex/gender binary framing may not capture full spectrum of biological and social variation; solutions remain largely aspirational.

## Citation
Cirillo et al. (2020). Sex and gender differences and biases in AI for biomedicine. npj Digit Med. doi:10.1038/s41746-020-0288-5
