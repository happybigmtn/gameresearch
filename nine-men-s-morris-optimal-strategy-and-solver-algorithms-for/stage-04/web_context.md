# Nine Men's Morris Strategy: Tips, Tactics & Openings
Nine Men's Morris is a classic two-player abstract strategy board game dating back to the Roman Empire. Form mills, three pieces in a row, to remove your opponent's pieces. The player who reduces their opponent to two pieces or blocks all their moves wins. This ancient game combines simple rules with deep strategic possibilities.

## Control the Intersections

The points where lines cross (T-junctions) are more valuable than corners because they connect to more positions. Controlling these gives you more mobility and options for forming mills.

## Form Double Mills

Try to create a configuration where moving one piece out of a mill immediately forms another mill. This allows you to remove an opponent's piece on every turn, quickly winning the game.

## Block Your Opponent

During the placing phase, watch for your opponent's potential mills and place pieces to block them. Preventing mills is just as important as forming your own.

## Preserve Your Pieces

Don't sacrifice pieces carelessly. You need at least 3 pieces to enter the flying phase and maintain a chance to win. Losing too many pieces early can be fatal.

## Maintain Mobility

Ensure your pieces have freedom to move. Getting blocked is a common way to lose even with more than two pieces remaining. Keep escape routes open.
